# User Stories

## US-001

**As an** Operations Manager

**I want to** receive a daily summary of critical operational issues

**So that** I can prioritize my team's work without manually checking multiple dashboards.

---

## US-002

**As a** Support Executive

**I want to** quickly retrieve a member's policy details and coverage information

**So that** I can answer customer queries without switching between multiple systems.

---

## US-003

**As a** Quality Analyst

**I want to** review customer interactions that received negative feedback

**So that** I can identify quality issues, provide feedback to support executives, and improve customer experience.

---

## US-004

**As an** Operations Associate

**I want to** identify dependencies between departments that are causing operational delays

**So that** I can proactively coordinate with the responsible teams and reduce turnaround time.

---

## US-005

**As an** Operations Trainer

**I want to** search and retrieve the latest SOPs and process documentation

**So that** I can train new employees using the most up-to-date operational guidelines.

---

## US-006

**As an** Operations Manager

**I want to** receive AI-powered recommendations on operational bottlenecks and resource allocation

**So that** I can proactively improve operational efficiency and optimize team capacity.

---

## US-007

**As a** Support Executive

**I want the AI to** explain operational comments, order updates, and case statuses in simple language

**So that** I can accurately communicate the current status to the customer.

---

## US-008

**As an** Operations Manager

**I want to** receive an AI-generated end-of-day operational summary along with the next day's workload prediction

**So that** I can plan staffing requirements and allocate resources more effectively.

---

## US-009

**As an** Operations Manager

**I want the AI to** automatically identify and prioritize critical SLA breaches and high-risk operational cases

**So that** my team can focus on resolving the most urgent issues first.
